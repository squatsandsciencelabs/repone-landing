RepOne Software Privacy Policy

We at Red Matter Labs, Inc. (“Red Matter Labs”) have created this privacy policy (this “Privacy Policy”) because we know that you care about how information you provide to us is used and shared. This Privacy Policy relates to the information collection and use practices of Red Matter Labs in connection with our hardware device (called “RepOne Device”), our apps available through the Apple App Store and the Google Play Store (collectively called “App”), and our virtual coaching service (referred to as “Service”). For the purposes of this document, the App and the virtual coaching interface are collectively called “Platform.”

By visiting our Website and/or using our Services on the Platform, you are agreeing to the terms of this Privacy Policy.

The Information We Collect

1. Personal Information

If you elect to sign in to the Platform using Google, you are authorizing Red Matter Labs to collect, store, and use, in accordance with this Privacy Policy, any and all information that you agreed that Google, Inc. (“Google”) could provide to Red Matter Labs through Google’s Application Programming Interface (“API”). Such information may include, without limitation, your identifier, access token, and e-mail address.

All information collected pursuant to this section is collectively referred to as “Personal Information.” We do not collect or access any Personal Information from you when you use the Services unless you provide us with the Personal Information voluntarily or authorize us to access it.

2. Lifting Metrics

The Platform provides you with high quality lifting data. In order to provide visualization of such data, we collect multiple metrics from the RepOne Device through BlueTooth. The metrics may include, without limitation, velocity, positional data, and range of motion. The Red Matter Labs Platform also calculates and collects relevant metrics, which may include, without limitation, rest times, set numbers, and rep numbers. All metrics mentioned in this section are collectively called the “Lifting Metrics.”

On Android specifically, location data is required for the BlueTooth connection to the RepOne Device to operate. We do not collect, store, or share the geolocation information of our users.

3. Workout Information

To help organize your Lifting Metrics, we collect additional information that you voluntarily provide to us. These include, without limitation, exercise names, RPE, weights, tags, and deleted reps. In addition, we also collect information about the Mobile Device that logged the Lifting Metrics, such as the platform (e.g., iOS or Android) and the version of the App. Such information is collectively called the “Workout Information.”

4. Activity Information

We may collect activity and usage information occurring via the Platform, collectively called the “Activity Information”. Such information may include, without limitation, modifying Settings, connecting to an RepOne Device, switching between tabs, editing a set, ending a set, ending a workout, using filters, and syncing with the cloud.

5. Other Information

We may collect additional information (collectively, the “Other Information”). Such Other Information may include tokens to assist in 3rd party services such as push notifications.

How We Use the Information

Red Matter Labs uses the data we collect to operate our business, provide our products and Services, improve existing products and services, develop new products and services, and to improve and personalize your experiences interacting with us. Some examples of the use of the data may include, without limitation:

  • Using Personal Information to allow our Platform to associate your data with your account.
  • With your permission, use Personal Information to assist you in your request (e.g., switching email addresses, updating data, removing data).
  • Applying Lifting Metrics and Workout Information to machine learning algorithms to test assumptions and to gain data science insights.
  • Using Activity Information to help guide future direction, design, and development.
  • Using Other Information to assist in 3rd party services such as push notifications.
